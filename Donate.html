Turtle conservation in Sri Lanka plays a crucial role in protecting and preserving the country's diverse marine ecosystem. Sri Lanka is home to several species of sea turtles, including the Olive Ridley, Green, Leatherback, Hawksbill, and Loggerhead turtles. These turtles face numerous threats, such as habitat degradation, pollution, illegal poaching, and accidental capture in fishing nets. To address these challenges, Sri Lanka has implemented various turtle conservation efforts. The country has established protected areas and hatcheries along its coastline where turtle eggs are collected, incubated, and later released into the ocean. These hatcheries provide a safe environment for turtle eggs to hatch and increase the survival rates of baby turtles. Additionally, local communities, conservation organizations, and government agencies work together to raise awareness about the importance of turtle conservation, promote sustainable fishing practices, and enforce regulations to protect nesting sites and turtle habitats. These collective efforts have contributed to the recovery and conservation of Sri Lanka's turtle populations, ensuring the long-term survival of these magnificent creatures.
