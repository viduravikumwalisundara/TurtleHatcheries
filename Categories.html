Turtle Categories

In Sri Lanka, several species of turtles can be found along its coastlines. The main categories or species of turtles in Sri Lanka

Olive Ridley Turtle (Lepidochelys olivacea)



Olive Ridley turtles are the most abundant species of sea turtles in Sri Lanka. They have a distinctive olive-colored carapace (shell) and are known for their synchronized nesting behavior called "arribadas," where large numbers of females come ashore simultaneously to lay their eggs.

Green Turtle (Chelonia mydas)



Green turtles are another common species found in Sri Lanka. They have a large, smooth carapace and are named for the green color of their fat and cartilage. They are herbivorous and feed primarily on seagrasses and algae.

Hawksbill Turtle (Eretmochelys imbricata)



Hawksbill turtles are known for their beautiful tortoiseshell-patterned carapace. They have a narrow, pointed beak, which helps them feed on sponges and other invertebrates. Hawksbill turtles are critically endangered and face threats such as habitat loss and illegal trade.

Loggerhead Turtle (Caretta caretta)



Loggerhead turtles are less commonly found in Sri Lanka compared to the other species. They have a large head and strong jaws, which they use to feed on hard-shelled prey such as crabs and mollusks. Loggerheads are listed as vulnerable and face various threats, including entanglement in fishing gear.

Leatherback Turtle (Dermochelys coriacea)



Leatherback turtles are the largest of all sea turtles and are known for their leathery carapace instead of a hard shell. They have a unique feeding adaptation, with their diet primarily consisting of jellyfish. Leatherback turtles are also classified as vulnerable, and their populations face threats such as habitat degradation and accidental capture.
