Turtle Threats

Turtles face numerous threats worldwide, including in Sri Lanka. Some of the common threats to turtles are

Habitat Loss and Degradation



Coastal development, pollution, and destruction of nesting beaches are major threats to turtle habitats. Human activities such as beachfront construction, sand mining, and coastal erosion can disrupt nesting sites and degrade critical foraging areas.

Illegal Wildlife Trade



Turtles are sought after for their shells, meat, and eggs, leading to illegal poaching and trade. The demand for turtle products, including traditional medicines and souvenirs, puts significant pressure on turtle populations.

Fishing Activities



Turtles often get accidentally caught in fishing gear such as trawls, nets, and longlines. This incidental capture, known as bycatch, can cause injury or death to turtles, particularly when they are unable to reach the surface for breathing.

Pollution



Marine pollution, including plastic debris, oil spills, and chemical pollutants, poses a significant threat to turtles. Ingestion of plastic or entanglement in debris can cause injury, internal blockages, and even death. Pollution can also disrupt the food chain, affecting the availability of prey for turtles.

Climate Change



Rising temperatures and sea levels, as well as changes in ocean currents, can impact turtle nesting sites and alter their foraging patterns. Climate change also affects the sex ratio of hatchlings, as the temperature during incubation determines their gender.

Predation and Natural Predators



Predators such as feral animals, birds, and crabs pose a threat to turtle eggs and hatchlings. Natural predators in the ocean, including sharks and large fish, can also prey on turtles.
